Answer by Tamoghna:

Sub Question 3:

How has she (Serena Williams) influenced society beyond athletics?

Serena Williams has influenced society way beyond athletics. She beat all of the racial and gender barriers to become a champion in tennis. She inspired many people to stand up to the obstacles in their way (like racism, sexism, e.t.c.) and she is a strong leader that has helped many people through charities, fundraisers, and many other things.

Some ways Serena Williams has helped and influenced society beyond athelitics:

  • Serena Williams supports various charities like Build African Schools, Common Ground Foundation, Elton John AIDS Foundation, Great Ormond Street Hospital, Hearts of Gold, Driving Force Giving Circle, I Heart My Girlfriends, The HollyRod Foundation, UNICEF and World Education.

  • Serena Williams is the founder of the Serena Williams Foundation, which donates to education, social welfare and community development. The foundation was established to facilitate education and community development.

  • In 2008, Serena Williams established the Serena Williams Secondary School in Matooni, Kenya. Williams is a Goodwill Ambassador of the United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) and has done extensive charity work with the organization since 2006.

  • She has also established the "Yetunde Price Nursing Scholarship" in association with the California Community Foundation. In 2010, Serena Williams and other tennis players gave up the final day of preparation for the 2010 Australian Open to raise funds for the Haiti earthquake victims.

  • In August 2020, Williams announced a partnership with Bella+Canvas, the National School Board Association, and Scholastic, to provide 4.25 million masks and educational materials to underserved schools in the United States; the #MasksForKids program would also donate a mask for each one purchased by the schools.
